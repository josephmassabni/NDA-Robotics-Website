The Bright Future
Over 6 years ago, the Notre Dame Academy program was first founded by motivated students, along with help from Mr. Adam Dietzo, the team's moderator. This club was a first for Notre Dame Academy, as it was primarily focused on STEM-Education, something not thought of at the time. These students had a vision for the future; sensing a missing piece in the clubs already offered at school, they decided to take matters in their own hands. Although the first year started off slowly, with only one team, 6001A, they were able to participate in two competitions: the Upper Midwest VEX Regional and the B.E.A.S.T. VRC State Qualifier. This newly-founded team was almost able to make it to the state competition in its first year, showing signs of greatness to come. Without the bold students of 2012, the Notre Dame Academy robotics program would not be here today.
With heavy recruitment throughout the school, news of the newrobotics team spread throughout the school. With a student population of less than 800, it was remarkable that the Robotics Team was able to inspire so many new students. The program quickly grew to multiple teams, and both were very successful, placing an emphasis over learning rather than success. These visionaries felt that success would come, and believed that it was most important to use these opportunities to learn more about the engineering process and the steps necessary to make a contender for the state championship. The year later, in 2014, the team was able to participate in 5 different competitions, including the state and national championship. Within 2 years of formation of the team, the Notre Dame Academy Tritons were able to compete with the best teams in the Greater Green Bay area.
Due to the quick success of the robotics program, the team was able to attract more and more talent. Due to their dedication to the engineering process and perfection on all fronts of the robot: the programming, design, and execution, the Tritons were able to finally compete in the VEX World Championships, for the first time in school history. This great experience proves how hard work and dedication can pay off, even if it is not evident in the moment. Following this immensely successful year, the robotics team was able to make it to the State Competition two more years in a row, including a Nationals appearance as well. DUring this time, expert programmers Noah and Jordan made a contribution to the school that our team members still use today: a custom API that was able to control the robots with an LCD, providing essential features such as a recordable autonomous. This revolutionary idea was called NDAPI, and differentiates our program from all other teams in the area.
Today, here in 2018, we are still greatful for all of the efforts of the past team members. It is our goal to carry on the success of those who put countless days into perfecting their craft. This year, there is not a single senior on our team. Although this means we do lack experience, we make up for it with our new and innovative ideas. We want to change the game that our predecessors enjoyed for the better, and represent our school even better than they have done. We sill benefit from the efforts of those who came before us, from our unique NDAPI to the multiple teachings created by students to help new members get started. Our goal for this year is to make it back to the State Championship, and hopefully even further. We will work harder than ever to make it happen, and feel that with enough effort, this goal is definitely managable.
The 2018-2019 season will be the last year that the NDA Robotics Program will be using the legacy VEX Cortex system. After this season, we will start transitioning to the brand-new V5 system, giving our team more options to innovate. With V5, the possibilities are endless. With this brand-new hardware, we feel that the State Championship is a very managable goal, and we want to bring as many of our teams as possible to the event. We will do everything in our power to make it happen, especially due to the fact that there will be many seniors on the team in the 2019-2020 season. Throughout our history, we have always pushed the limits. This program was started by those who refused to submit to the restrictions placed on them, instead taking a risk to build something never-before-seen in school history. That is how greatness is made.

How has VEX Robotics affected our students?



To us, VEX Robotics stands for more than just an event where we are able to build something and compete: it is a lifestyle. This program emphasizes what real life is all about. Instead of conforming to the boundaries already reached by others, we must reach beyond them and make new discoveries, for the benefits of all of society. In real life, challenges will be faced, and they will seem to be almost impossible to overcome. However, with enough persistence, it will become evident that these obstacles were simply bumps in the road, and an experience to learn something new.



The VEX Robotics Competitions have helped our students to go beyond the classroom and learn real-life skills of collaboration, problem-solving, risk-taking, and making decisions under pressure. These situations are faced during every competition, as well as while planning and building our robots to compete with.



Secondly, our school has gained a program that prepares students for the increasingly tech-heavy world that will await them after graduation. Among one of the only available STEM-oriented clubs at Notre Dame Academy, our Robotics Team has successfully inspired students to pursue STEM-related topics of study in college and beyond.



Lastly, we are forever greatful to the REC Foundation for its endless support, providing grants to participate in as many competitions as possible and to purchase innovative parts that make our robots able to perform any idea that we have envisioned.
